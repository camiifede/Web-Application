Pantheon

Greek mythology is foundational to ancient Greek religion and culture, showcasing the complex interactions between gods and humans within its expansive pantheon and intricate stories. The mythology centers around twelve key Olympian gods: Zeus, Poseidon, Hera, Demeter, Aphrodite, Athena, Artemis, Apollo, Ares, Hephaestus, Hermes, and Hestia—or Dionysus in some versions. These gods, residing on Mount Olympus, are pivotal in mythology, shaping all facets of ancient life.




Our website goes into great detail on these Olympian gods. Each had its own functionaries: the Horae as attendants to Zeus, the Muses to Apollo, Eilithyia and Hebe to Hera, the Erotes to Aphrodite, showing the configuration of divine power.

Furthermore, we explore the Titan gods, including the twelve Uranides—Cronus, Oceanus, Iapetus, Hyperion, Crius, Coeus, Rhea, Tethys, Theia, Phoebe, Themis, and Mnemosyne—and the four Iapetionides: Atlas, Prometheus, Epimetheus, and Menoetius. These figures, along with their progeny like the Hyperionides (Helius, Selene, and Eos), the Coeides (Leto, Asteria, and Hecate), and the Creionides (Pallas, Astraeus, and Perses), create an extensive family tree central to many myths.
